Founder Radar

A 30-second tour.

1 · What this is

Founder Radar watches public signals (job changes, new repos, research papers, Show HN posts, follows from trusted scout accounts) and ranks people who look like they are about to start a company.

2 · How a score is built

Recent, strong signals add more points. Older signals fade out over weeks; that fading is the "decay". Different signal types carry different point values; those are the "weights". Click any score to see its receipt.

The full math, if you want it
score = min(99, round( Σ weight × 0.5^(age_days / 21) × quality_prior + x_check ))

Signals: weight, how each is tracked, and what is wired

Recency decay

Each signal decays with a 21-day half-life: full weight the day it fires, half after three weeks, a quarter after six. A person is interesting because of what happened recently, so old signals fade instead of accumulating forever.

Founder-quality prior (multiplier)

X check (optional adjustment) WIRED

Runs automatically for the top 10 real candidates each day (and from the posts check in the X numbers panel), against the live X API: cadence min(4, posts/week) + audience min(4, log10(followers)) + engagement (2 if median likes ≥ 10, 1 if ≥ 3), cap +10. Only real profiles get X numbers; sample candidates have none.

Scores cap at 99. "Likely" means 60 or above. Live Hacker News and watched-follow candidates run through the identical engine.
