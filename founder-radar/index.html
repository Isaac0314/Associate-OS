Founder Radar

A 30-second tour.

1 · What this is

Founder Radar watches public signals (job changes, new repos, preprints, Show HN posts) and ranks people who look like they are about to start a company.

2 · How a score is built

Recent, strong signals add more points. Older signals fade out over weeks; that fading is the "decay". Different signal types carry different point values; those are the "weights". Click any score to see its receipt.

3 · Who writes what

Nothing here is written by the model. Numbers come from code, the model only drafts prose, and you send everything yourself.

The full math, if you want it
score = min(99, round( Σ weight × 0.5^(age_days / 21) × quality_prior + x_check ))

Signal weights

Recency decay

Each signal decays with a 21-day half-life: full weight the day it fires, half after three weeks, a quarter after six. A person is interesting because of what happened recently, so old signals fade instead of accumulating forever.

Founder-quality prior (multiplier)

X check (optional adjustment)

Runs from the X button next to a candidate's name: cadence min(4, posts/week) + audience min(4, log10(followers)) + engagement (2 if median likes ≥ 10, 1 if ≥ 3), cap +10. Sample numbers exist only for the fictional candidates and are labeled as such.

Scores cap at 99. "Likely" means 60 or above. Live Hacker News candidates run through the identical engine.
